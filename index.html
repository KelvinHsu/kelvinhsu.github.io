Brief vitae

I am PhD student at the CEREMADE of Paris-Dauphine University, working under the supervision of Cyril Labbé. I completed the Master programme M2 Probailité et Modèle Aléatoires at the Pierre et Marie Curie University (Paris-VI) in 2020. I hold also a Bachelor double degree in Mathematics and Electrical Engineering from the National Taiwan University, awarded in 2018.

My primary research interest is stochastic partial differenctial equations (SPDE), more specifically the parabolic Anderson model (PAM) and the Anderson hamiltonian.
